A large, opaque resale market for frontier LLM APIs has emerged to serve users blocked from OpenAI, Anthropic and Google endpoints (somewhat like a VPN). Two independent papers in the past two months provide the first systematic evidence that a substantial fraction of these intermediaries silently **substitute models, truncate context, or misreport billing**.

Apart from vast deception, the shadow market maybe more chaotic and creates a lot of loss than ideal: for example, router providers would scam by using fake educational account. Or when Anthropic started ID-verification, routers pivoted to poor countries to get headshots and fake ID for logins for a few dollars from locals. For users, using a shitty model is fine but sometimes the shadow AI-agents are intentionally seeding malicious software on users. Not to mention data privacy.

It's all just hearsay — but, given the (repugnant) market's size and profitability (mainly software engineering) — why am I not surprised?

Some interesting reference to look into:

[1] Zhang, Y., Jiang, Y., Chen, Z., Backes, M., Shen, X., Zhang, Y. *Real Money, Fake Models: Deceptive Model Claims in Shadow APIs.* CISPA Helmholtz Center, arXiv:2603.01919v2, 5 Mar 2026.

[2] Lin, G., Wan, Y., Pei, S., Xu, T., Xu, K., Xue, G. *Behavioral Consistency and Transparency Analysis on Large Language Model API Gateways.* IMC '26, Karlsruhe; arXiv:2604.21083v1, 22 Apr 2026.

[3] APPSO. *"AI scalpers — how deep is this lucrative business?"* 8 May 2026. https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/SmmAVhILzwASP8tpuNGEKw
